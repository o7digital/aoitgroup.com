The Power of Technology: New Ideas for Leaders of Growing Businesses

Tom_McDowall October 2025 • 5 min read

As a technology leader in a growing organisation, you're constantly juggling competing priorities. You need to drive innovation, ensure robust security, and scale your infrastructure – all while working with limited budgets and lean teams. The pressure to 'do more with less' is relentless.

But what happens when rapid growth outpaces your resources? Can your current systems handle the increased workload? Are you confident in your ability to protect against increasingly sophisticated cyber threats? A single misstep can cripple your operations, damage your reputation, and derail your growth trajectory.

That's where a strategic approach to technology comes in. By leveraging automation, AI, cloud solutions, and robust cybersecurity, you can unlock unprecedented efficiency, gain a competitive edge, and build a resilient foundation for sustainable growth. This article pulls together actionable strategies and transformative ideas to empower you to confidently lead your business into 2026.

At Corsaire, we understand that successful technology enhancements are about more than just adopting new software; it's about building a resilient, agile, and secure operational foundation. Our expertise lies in guiding businesses through this journey with precision and reliability.

1. Unlocking Efficiency Through Automation

As the one responsible for technology at your organisation, you're likely feeling the pressure to optimise every aspect of your operations. But you don't have the luxury of throwing bodies at every problem. That's where strategic automation comes in. It's not just about saving time; it's about unlocking hidden potential, freeing up your team's bandwidth for strategic initiatives, and gaining a competitive edge.
 

Beyond the Basics: Thinking Differently About Automation

While basic automation (like invoicing and payroll) is essential, truly transformative automation goes further. It's about identifying high-impact, repetitive tasks that are currently consuming valuable time and resources and then designing smart solutions to streamline them. Here are a few less obvious areas we think all businesses should be considering:

  • Knowledge Management: Stop letting valuable knowledge walk out the door every time an employee leaves. Implement a system to automatically document and organise key processes, procedures, and best practices.
    • Actionable Insight: Tools like Notion or Microsoft’s CoPilot can automate the creation of process documentation based on employee activity, reducing onboarding time and ensuring consistency.
       
  • Security Incident Response: Don't wait for a security breach to scramble. Automate your initial incident response process to quickly identify, contain, and mitigate threats.
    • ​Actionable Insight: Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) systems like Azure Sentinel can automatically detect suspicious activity, trigger alerts, and even initiate automated remediation steps.
       
  • IT Support Ticket Routing: Streamline your IT support process by automatically routing tickets to the appropriate technician based on the nature of the issue and the employee's skill set. 
    • Actionable Insight: Tools like FreshService can use AI-powered routing to ensure that tickets are resolved quickly and efficiently, improving employee satisfaction and reducing downtime.

 

The "Automation Audit": A Three-Step Process

  1. Identify the Bottlenecks: Conduct a thorough review of your key business processes to identify areas where manual tasks are slowing things down. Talk to your team members to understand their pain points.

  2. Prioritise the Opportunities: Focus on automating the tasks that have the biggest impact on your business goals. Consider factors like time savings, cost reduction, and improved accuracy.

  3. Choose the Right Tools: Select automation tools that are scalable, reliable, and easy to use. Look for solutions that integrate with your existing systems.
     

Don't Just Automate; Optimise

Automation is a powerful tool, but it's not a magic bullet. To truly unlock its potential, you need to continuously monitor and optimise your automated processes. Track key metrics, gather feedback from your team, and make adjustments as needed.

2. Gaining a Competitive Edge with Artificial Intelligence (AI)

AI isn't just for tech giants - you can leverage the power of AI but to succeed, you need to move beyond the hype and focus on practical AI applications that deliver real business value.

7%

Less than 1 in 10 emerging businesses are actively using AI technologies

Source: McKinsey

 

Unlocking Hidden Potential: AI Applications You Haven't Considered

While chatbots and basic predictive analytics are a good starting point, here are some less-obvious AI applications that can transform your workplace:

  • AI-Powered Threat Detection: Go beyond traditional security measures with AI-powered threat detection systems that can identify and neutralise sophisticated cyberattacks in real-time.
    • Actionable Insight: Solutions like Darktrace use machine learning to analyse network traffic and user behaviour, identifying anomalies that indicate a potential breach. This helps you proactively defend against ransomware, phishing attacks, and other advanced threats.
       
  • Intelligent Document Processing (IDP): Automate the extraction and processing of data from unstructured documents like invoices, contracts, and emails.
    • Actionable Insight: Tools like Power Automate can significantly reduce manual data entry, improve accuracy, and accelerate business processes. Imagine automatically extracting key information from hundreds of invoices per day, freeing up your finance team for more strategic tasks.
       
  • AI-Driven Sales Forecasting: Improve the accuracy of your sales forecasts by leveraging AI algorithms that analyse historical data, market trends, and customer behaviour.
    • Actionable Insight: Platforms like Gong or Clari use AI to analyse sales calls, identify winning strategies, and provide real-time coaching to sales reps. Optimise your sales process, close more deals, and achieve your revenue targets.

 

The "AI Adoption Framework": A Practical Guide for Growing Organisations

  1. Start with a Business Problem: Don't implement AI for the sake of AI. Spend time defining the specific business problem that you want to solve.
  2. Gather the Right Data: AI algorithms need data to learn. Ensure that you have access to high-quality, relevant data.
  3. Monitor and Evaluate: Continuously monitor the performance of your AI solutions and make adjustments as needed.

 

The Democratisation of AI: Affordable Solutions for Any Business

AI is now more accessible and affordable than ever before. The wide range of AI services that exist means you can easily integrate them into your existing applications and business processes – which is good, because the future of your business may depend on it!

3. Fortifying your Business: Cybersecurity as a Growth Enabler, Not Just a Cost

You almost certainly understand that cybersecurity is no longer just about protecting your data; it's about protecting your reputation, your customers, and your future growth. In today's threat landscape, a proactive and strategic approach to cybersecurity is a business imperative, not just an IT checklist. But how do you build a robust security posture without breaking the bank or overwhelming your team?

 

81%

UK businesses targeted by phishing attacks in 2024

Source: KnowBe4 Phishing by Industry Benchmarking Report

 

Beyond the Basics: Cybersecurity Strategies for Organisations On A Budget

While employee training, managed security services, and data encryption are essential, here are some less-conventional cybersecurity strategies that can give you a competitive edge:

  • Cybersecurity Mesh Architecture: Instead of relying on a traditional, perimeter-based security model, embrace a distributed approach that places security controls closer to the assets they are designed to protect. 
    • Actionable Insight: Implement microsegmentation to isolate critical applications and data. Use identity-based access control to restrict access to sensitive resources. This limits the impact of a breach and makes it more difficult for attackers to move laterally within your network.
       
  • Security Automation and Orchestration: Automate repetitive security tasks like threat detection, incident response, and vulnerability management to free up your security team and improve their efficiency. 
    • Actionable Insight: Integrate your security tools with a SOAR platform to automate incident response workflows. Azure Sentinel is great if you are already using Microsoft 365 tools for your security. This allows you to quickly contain and remediate threats, minimising downtime and data loss.
       
  • Breach and Attack Simulation: Proactively test your security defences by simulating real-world attacks. This helps you identify vulnerabilities and weaknesses in your security posture before attackers can exploit them. 
    • Actionable Insight: Consider using a BAS tools like AttackIQ to simulate different attack scenarios and assess the effectiveness of your security controls. If BAS tools aren’t your thing, we love running table-top exercises with our clients as they take very little time to set up and can generate a new level of focus on Cybersecurity for our clients.

 

The "Cybersecurity Maturity Model": A Roadmap for Continuous Improvement

  1. Assess Your Current State: Use a cybersecurity maturity model (we use NIST Frameworks at A&O Corsaire) to assess your current security posture – a great technology partner can help with this!
  2. Identify Your Gaps: Identify the areas where you are falling short of your desired security maturity level.
  3. Develop a Roadmap: Create a roadmap for continuous improvement, prioritising the areas that have the biggest impact on your business risk.
  4. Measure Your Progress: Track your progress against your roadmap and make adjustments as needed.

 

The ROI of Cybersecurity: Protecting Your Bottom Line

Investing in cybersecurity isn't just about avoiding fines and reputational damage; it's about protecting your bottom line. The best examples in this space are able to turn great cybersecurity practices into a tool to win new business, retain existing customers, and attract top talent.
